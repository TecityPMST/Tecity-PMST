State of the MOU
On Day 20 of 60, the ceasefire is holding but the deal stays on pause. Iran is in the final days of state funeral ceremonies for Ayatollah Khamenei (4–9 Jul, burial 9 Jul), and negotiations remain suspended for the mourning period; Qatar reports the last Doha round made “positive progress” and says the US–Iran track will reconvene “at the earliest possible time” once the funeral ends, with reporting pointing to a restart around 11 Jul covering sanctions, frozen funds and the still-unopened nuclear file. The US–Iran stand-down has now held roughly eleven days with no new strikes between them, but the Lebanon front (Art 1) stays active and keeps the ceasefire clause in breach territory: the IDF says it has seized Hezbollah tunnel networks near Beaufort Castle and warns of a “swift offensive” if the truce breaks, and an unclaimed attack hit a bulk carrier off Houthi-held Hudaydah. The nuclear file (Art 8) stays barely touched — Grossi insists IAEA access to enrichment sites is “going to happen” while Tehran ties any access to a final deal. Hormuz (Art 5) is recovering — a first day back within the pre-war tanker range — but weekly volume is still below baseline, and Iran now floats “special considerations” (discounts) for “friendly” states on post-window “service fees” even as US envoys press it not to impose tolls; ISW assesses Iran is prioritizing recognition of its control of the strait over toll revenue. Oil (Art 10) stays the clearest delivered item — GL X runs to 21 Aug and exports are back near pre-war levels. Frozen funds (Art 11) remain unconfirmed, with Qatar still calling the ~$6B frozen even as Tehran says a tranche will be freed. Analysts widely expect the 60-day window (Art 3, ends ~16 Aug) to be extended by mutual consent. The six status counts are unchanged from the prior refresh.
